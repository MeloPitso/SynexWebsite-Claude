01 — What It Is

Documents in,
structured data out

Every business handles documents. Contracts, invoices, application forms, compliance submissions, ID documents, financial statements, certificates. Most businesses handle them the same way: someone reads each one, finds the relevant information, and manually enters it somewhere else. It's slow, it's tedious, and it's error-prone in exactly the ways that matter most.

Document processing automation changes this entirely. We build pipelines that receive your documents — whether they arrive by email, upload, or shared drive — and automatically extract the key data: names, dates, amounts, clauses, signatures, references. That data is validated, structured, and sent directly where it needs to go. No manual reading. No manual entry. No delays.

The technology that makes this possible combines optical character recognition (OCR) with large language models — meaning it doesn't just read printed text, it understands context. It can identify that a number on page 3 is a contract value, not a reference number. It can flag clauses that don't match your standard template. It can extract data from scanned PDFs, handwritten forms, or complex multi-page documents that would take a human 20 minutes to process.

02 — What You're Losing Without It

Manual processing is where
accuracy goes to die

The true cost of manual document processing isn't just time — it's accuracy. When a person reads through a 40-page contract and manually captures key terms, they will miss something. That's not a criticism; it's biology. Humans are poor at sustained, repetitive extraction tasks. The system, however, doesn't get tired on page 30.

Think about what a single error in a contract review costs — a missed clause, an incorrect rate captured from an invoice, a compliance form submitted with the wrong date. These aren't hypothetical risks. They're the everyday reality of businesses that haven't automated this process yet.

And then there's the backlog. When documents pile up faster than your team can process them, decisions get delayed. A property transfer stalls because the FICA documents are sitting in an inbox unreviewed. A finance application sits incomplete because the supporting documents haven't been extracted and filed. The bottleneck isn't the work itself — it's the pace at which humans can manually handle it.

03 — Use Case

A real story worth
telling

Case Study
Pretoria Legal Firm — Conveyancing Practice

A mid-size legal firm in Pretoria was receiving 60–80 client documents a week across their conveyancing practice — FICA packs, title deeds, transfer documents, and bond applications. Each pack required an admin to open every document, extract key information, and manually populate the client record in their case management system.

An experienced admin could process about 15 packs a day. The rest piled up. On busy weeks, clients were waiting two to three days just for their documents to be captured — before any legal work had even started.

We built a document processing pipeline that monitors their intake inbox, extracts key fields from each document type automatically, validates the data against their compliance checklist, and populates the case management system directly. Average processing time per pack dropped from 25 minutes to under 90 seconds. The team now focuses entirely on the legal work — the documents just happen.
